Travel to Kazakhstan

Main info about country

The Republic of Kazakhstan is a unitary state with the presidential system of government, which gained independence on December 16, 1991. It is divided into 17 administrative zones and populated by about 130 ethnic groups - namely by Kazakhs, Russians, Uzbeks, Ukrainians, Uighurs, Tatars, Germans and others.

The most modern cities:

  • Astana

    • Astana, formerly known as Nur-Sultan, Akmolinsk, Tselinograd, and Akmola, is the capital and second-largest city of Kazakhstan with a population of 1,350,228 within the city limits after Almaty, which had been the capital until 1997.

  • Almaty

    • Almaty remains the largest, most developed, and most ethnically and culturally diverse city in Kazakhstan. Due to development by the Soviet Union and relocation of workers and industries from European areas of the Soviet Union during World War II, the city has a high proportion of ethnic Russians and Uyghurs.

  • Shymkent

    • Shymkent is a city in southern Kazakhstan, located near the border with Uzbekistan. It holds the status of a city of republican significance, one of only three cities in Kazakhstan with this distinction, alongside Almaty and Astana.

  • Aktau

    • Aktau is one of the most fascinating and unusual cities in Kazakhstan. Its name is translated from Kazakh as "the white mountain." The city lives up to its name because it stands on a white limestone rock. Aktau is best known for its beaches and the sea; however, it can offer much more.

List of beautiful and historical places in Kazakhstan :

Natural Wonders :

  1. Charyn Canyon

    2. Known as the "Grand Canyon of Central Asia," it features stunning rock formations and colorful landscapes.

  2. Kolsai Lakes

    3. A series of alpine lakes surrounded by pine forests, ideal for hiking and camping.

  3. Kaindy Lake

    4. Famous for its submerged forest with visible tree trunks rising from the water.

  4. Borovoe National Park (Burabay)

    5. A picturesque region with lakes, forests, and rock formations, often called the "Switzerland of Kazakhstan."

  5. Altyn-Emel National Park

    6. Home to the "Singing Dune," a sand dune that emits a melodic sound when the wind blows.

  6. Tien Shan Mountains

    7. A stunning mountain range perfect for trekking, skiing, and climbing.

  7. Caspian Sea Coast

    8. Beautiful beaches and a great place to relax by the sea.

Cultural and Historical Sites in Kazakhstan :

  • Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi

    A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Turkestan, an important Islamic and architectural monument.

  • Tamgaly Petroglyphs

    A UNESCO-listed site featuring ancient rock carvings dating back thousands of years.

  • Alzhir Memorial Museum

    A museum near Astana dedicated to the victims of political repression.

  • Otrar Ruins

    The remains of an ancient Silk Road city with rich history.

  • Aisha Bibi Mausoleum

    A beautifully ornate mausoleum symbolizing eternal love.

Modern Attractions :

Astana (Nur-Sultan) :

  • Bayterek Tower: A symbol of the city offering panoramic views.
  • Khan Shatyr: A giant transparent tent housing shops and entertainment.
  • Hazret Sultan Mosque: One of the largest mosques in Central Asia.
  • Palace of Peace and Reconciliation: A pyramid-shaped building for cultural events.

Almaty :

  • Medeu Ice Rink: The world's highest outdoor skating rink.
  • Shymbulak Ski Resort: A popular skiing destination.
  • Green Bazaar: A bustling market with local food and souvenirs.
  • Kok-Tobe Hill: A recreational area with views of the city and mountains.

Unique Experiences :

  • Baikonur Cosmodrome

    The world's first and largest space launch facility, where you can watch rocket launches.

  • Ethno-Villages

    Experience Kazakh traditions, nomadic yurts, and traditional cuisine.

  • Falconry Shows

    Witness the ancient art of hunting with trained eagles.

Adventure Activities :

  • Trekking and Hiking: Popular routes in the Tien Shan and Altai Mountains.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the vast steppe landscapes on horseback, as nomads have done for centuries.
  • Desert Exploration: Visit the Betpak-Dala desert or the Mangystau region for unique desert landscapes.

Cities and Urban Attractions :

  • Astana (Nur-Sultan): A showcase of modern Kazakhstan with innovative architecture and cultural sites.
  • Almaty: A vibrant city blending cosmopolitan life with breathtaking nature.
  • Shymkent: A historical city with vibrant markets and nearby archaeological sites.
  • Aktau: A gateway to the Caspian Sea with unique desert-meets-sea landscapes.

Kazakh Culture and Cuisine :

  • Traditional Food

    Try beshbarmak (boiled meat with noodles), kumis (fermented mare's milk), and baursaks (fried dough).

  • Cultural Festivals

    Experience Nauryz, the Kazakh New Year celebrated in March, featuring traditional games, food, and music.
