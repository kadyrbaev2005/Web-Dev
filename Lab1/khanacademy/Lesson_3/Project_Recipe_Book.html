Recipe #1 for Black Cod with Miso

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Black cod fillets 4 pieces(about 6 oz each)
Sake ¼ cup
Mirin ¼ cup
White miso paste ½ cup
Granulated sugar ⅓ cup

Steps for preparing:

Step 1: Prepare the marinade by cooking sake, mirin, miso paste, and sugar until dissolved and cooled.

Step 2: Coat the black cod fillets with the marinade, cover, and refrigerate for 2-3 days.

Step 3: When ready to cook, wipe off excess marinade, broil the fillets until caramelized, and bake in the oven until cooked through.

Source: Just One Dish: Nobu


Recipe #2 for Stanley Tucci's Tuna Melt Sandwich

  • Time: 15 minutes
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
Canned white tuna (Albacore) 1 can (5-6 oz), drained
Red onion 2 tablespoons, finely diced
Celery 2 tablespoons, finely diced
Mayonnaise 2 tablespoons (Tucci prefers Hellmann's)
Wheat or sourdough bread 2 slices
Emmental cheese 2 slices
Butter for spreading

Steps for preparing:

Step 1: In a bowl, mix drained tuna, mayonnaise, diced onions, and celery, seasoning with salt and pepper.

Step 2: Spread the tuna salad onto a slice of bread, layer it with cheese, and top with another slice.

Step 3: Butter the outer sides of the sandwich and grill in a skillet or panini press until the bread is golden brown and the cheese is melted.

Source: Stanley Tucci's 'Genius' Sandwich Is the Easy, No-Brainer Recipe You Need


Recipe #3 for Puerto Rican Pernil al Caldero (Pork Roast)

  • Time: Approximately 4-5 hours (excluding marinating time).
  • Serves: 6-8
Ingredients Quantity
Pork shoulder or ham 4-5 lbs, with skin on
Garlic cloves 6-8, minced
Dried oregano 2 teaspoons
Olive oil 2 tablespoons
Salt and pepper to taste
White vinegar 2 tablespoons
Water 1 cup

Steps for preparing:

Step 1: Make a garlic-oregano paste by combining minced garlic, oregano, salt, pepper, olive oil, and vinegar.

Step 2: Rub the paste into deep slits made on the pork shoulder, cover, and refrigerate to marinate for several hours or overnight.

Step 3: Roast the pork in a covered pot with water until tender, then uncover for the last 30 minutes to crisp the skin.

Source: This Puerto Rican pork roast boasts tender meat and crackling, crispy skin
